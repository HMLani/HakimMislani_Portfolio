'Back to present' Virtual Reality (VR) Escape Room

'Back to Present' is a Virtual Reality (VR) Escape Room game that I've made using Unity. The process of developing it challenging as I had to think of a theme and puzzles that not too simple or too difficult for players to solve when playing the game. The theme idea was that the player accidentally fell into a portal of thier future self and to escape the lab while solving puzzles in each rooms. The puzzles in each rooms are unique and different from each other as I wanted each rooms to have a different feeling and experience rather than using the same puzzles mechanics and dull gameplay throughout the game. Each rooms has a tablet of what to do so that the player knows how to proceed out the room.

Pluzzes featured in game

Room 1: Player has to place cubes on each sets of 3 'pressure plate' to leave and procceed to the next room. The 'pressure plate' has no animation when the cube is placed on it but there will be a sound indication when the cubes onto of the plates. The first set of cubes are place near the plates to let the player know what to do but the other cubes are in the room when the player has to find them.

Room 2: Player has to key in the correct number combination to unlock the door. The number combination for the puzzle is shown by the light pads near the number pad terminal. Each light pad is light up to the combination of terminal and players had to press the correct number of the light position to proceed to the next room. For example, if the lights position are 1, 4, 5, 9, the player has to press 1, 4, 5, 9 on the number terminal. There are 3 terminals with different combinations to be solved and a sound will be played when all terminals are entered correctly.

Room 3: Player has to enter a combination of the correct flashing light sequence on one of the 4 terminals in the middle of the room. The clue is shown on the tablet and have to look at the number of flashes on the light where the tablet stated to open the door. If the player entered the number sequence of flashing lights on the wrong terminal, a sound will indicate that the player enter it in the wrong terminal but if the player enter it on the correct terminal, a sound will play and the door will to let the player proceed to the next room.

Room 4: Player has to look around the room to find a colour tube to place on top of 'table' where the tube will automatically placed. The tube must be placed on the correct 'pad' to open the door and if the player placed it on the wrong pad on the 'table', the light in front of the table will light up red with a sound it and vice versa. When all six tubes are placed correctly, the door will open and the player will be able to escape the lab.

Name Card Design

The proces to create this name card was quite a process as I have to think of several design concepts that look minimalistic and professional for each elemetns to be seen clearly for viewer to see. The choice of the color palette was chosen as to make it pop for better visibility and appealing if it's printed into a physical busniess card. The goal of why I chose the color was that I wanted to make the card feel calm and pleasing to the viwers eyes and not too flashy and cluttered with too much design elements as want to focused on clarity and readibility maintaining a layout that is clean and organized. On the back of the card, I've decided to make it clean and simple with my logo positioned to the right slightly rather than to the center as I wanted to make it look apart from other standard card design that I've encountered during my research when designing ideas.

The font used are 'ITC Avant Garde Gothic' for both my name and my designated job title for the viewer focus on when they either take a quick glance or skim through it with the font sizes of 10 to 11 points and font weight in bold and regular. As for my information details, I used 'Futara PT' as it's looks clean, modern and easy to read in font size of 10 points and font weight bold and light. The logo idea was created by using my first two letter of my first and last name combined into each other. I've decided to make it look like a tattoo design as it look unique and different.
